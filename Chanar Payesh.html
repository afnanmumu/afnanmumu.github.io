Chanar Payesh

Posted on 20 October 2020

Channar Payesh is an authentic dessert recipe of Bengali cuisine that you can make for your family and friends on special occasions and festivals. Also known as cottage cheese pudding or paneer kheer, this is an exotic and classic sweet dish which is quite popular during the festive season. The best thing about this kheer recipe is that you don't need to add sugar in it, it is prepared using condensed milk which itself is sweet enough to make the dish taste sweeter. This dessert recipe is cooked using paneer or cottage cheese, milk, condensed milk, and uses green cardamom powder that enhances the taste and flavour of this mouth-watering dish. Try it at home and enjoy with your loved ones!

Ingredients:

  • Paneer or Chana from 1 and half liters of milk.
  • 4 Tablespoons of condensed milk or 1/4th cup of sugar.
  • 1 liter of full fat milk.
  • 2 pieces of green cardamom.
  • 1 Tablespoon of chopped pistachios.
  • Saffron - 8-10 pieces

Steps:

  1. Heat full fat milk in a sauce pan.
  2. On medium heat stir often and boil until the milk becomes half of its quantity.
  3. Now take the paneer in a plate and crumble it with your hand. Do not make it very smooth.
  4. You can use home made paneer or store bought one for this recipe. Just make sure that the paneer should be dry. Otherwise milk will get curdled.
  5. For the safety, add 3-4 Tablespoons of hot milk in crumbled paneer, mix well.
  6. Now add the entire paneer into the reduced milk, mix well.
  7. Then add the condensed milk and green cardamom, mix well and cook on low flame for 10-12 minutes.
  8. When the kheer will become semi thick, turn off the heat. Do make it very thick like Rice kheer.
  9. Let it come to room temperature, then keep it in fridge for 1 hour.
  10. Before serving, garnish it with chopped pistachio.
