Sitabhog

Posted on 20 October 2020

Sita Bhog is my choice for S in the A to Z Bengali Sweets Series. I don’t think I came across any other Bengali Sweet starting with S that prompted me to select. I first read about this in a “Must eat Bengali Sweets” list and very reluctantly chose this, hoping I would get another dish for S. As luck would have it, I couldn’t find anything else with S. So I decided to attempt Sita Bhog and procrastinated till the end as this was the last one in the series to get cooked. The dish really put me off with the seemingly complex steps involved. I was just not able to wrap the recipe around my mind. Maybe it could do with the way the recipe was explained.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Paneer / Chhana / Cottage Cheese
  • 2 tsp Rice Powder
  • 1 to 2 tsp Milk as needed in the final stage of kneading
  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 1 Cardamom
  • Few Strands Saffron
  • 1 cup Milk Powder
  • 1/2 cup All Purpose Flour / Maida
  • A Pinch Baking Soda
  • 1.5 tsp Ghee
  • 2 tbsp Curds / Yogurt
  • 1 cup Sugar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 Cardamom
  • Few Strands Saffron
  • Ghee for deep frying, I made a mix of cooking oil and ghee.

Steps:

  1. Soak 1 cup of Rice in water for a couple of hours. Drain and spread over a kitchen towel and keep under the fan to make it completely dry. Once dry, you can powder it to a fine powder. The rice might still be little damp, which will help in holding the dish shape. Alternatively, you can use store bought Rice flour as well. I used store bought rice flour.
  2. Make the soft fresh chhana for preparing Bengali Sweets. Take the portion needed to make this sweet.
  3. Knead the chhana for about 10 to 15 mins, till you see the chhana oozing out fat and looks shiny.
  4. Gather the cheese and add the rice flour. Knead again till the rice flour is well incorporated and the cheese holds its shape.
  5. Sprinkle the milk and knead the cheese into a ball again.
  6. Using the grater, first, try out grating through it to check if you are getting fine vermicelli size strings.
  7. Heat the Kadai with oil and ghee mix, when it is hot, simmer and keep the grater directly over it and grate the cheese ball into the oil
  8. You will get fine strings of cheese that will get cooked very quickly. So make sure you keep an eye on that to make sure oil is not very hot.
  9. Drain with a slotted spoon.
  10. Melt the sugar in water and remove impurities if any, add cardamom and saffron and bring to a boil.
  11. Once the water starts rolling boil, let it get cooked for another 5 to 7 mins. We are not looking for a string consistency. Only for the sita bhog cheese strings to soak the sweet.
  12. Gently drop the cooked cheese strings into the sugar syrup, close with a lid and let it dunk all the sweetness. This could take about an hour depending on the texture. Drain the strings and spread on a plate to dry out.
  13. In another bowl, add Milk powder, Maida, Soda, Ghee, and curd. Mix well and knead it for some 10 to 15 mins. Ensure the dough is soft and does not crack.
  14. Pinch out tiny balls and shape as puli or oval shape.
  15. Heat oil and ghee mix in a Kadai. When the oil is hot, simmer to low flame, drop in the Nikhuti in batches and deep fry till they turn golden brown colour.
  16. Drain using a slotted spoon and keep it aside.
  17. Since I had the sugar syrup excess from the previous batch of sita bhog, I simply added extra sugar and made the syrup in the same pan.
  18. When the syrup is done, drop the Nikhutis into it and let it soak in sugar syrup for an hour or so.
  19. I made more Nikhutis and served as such. These made the perfect mini gulab jamuns.
  20. Drain the Nikhutis from the syrup and mix to the sita bhog and toss it to get mixed well.
  21. Serve it as chilled or at room temperature as a dessert.
